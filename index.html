​**What are Tape Measures and Rulers?**

Tape measures and rulers are key for measuring stuff in all sorts of jobs—like building things, sewing, schoolwork, art, and making things.

A tape measure is like a bendy ruler to find out how big something is or how far away things are.

A ruler is stiff and straight. It's great for drawing lines or measuring short lengths.

**Types of Rulers**

1.  **Regular Ruler:**

    *   About 6 to 12 inches long.
    *   Made of plastic, wood, or metal.
    *   Good for school, the office, and simple drawings.
2.  **Yardstick:**

    *   A bit less than 1 yard long.
    *   For general measuring, in class, or for projects.
3.  **Architect's Scale Ruler:**

    *   Triangle-shaped with different scales.
    *   For drawing buildings and making models.
4.  **Engineer's Scale Ruler:**

    *   Looks like the architect's one, but for different units (inches and feet).
5.  **Flexible Rulers:**

    *   Can bend around stuff.
    *   Perfect for sewing and measuring curves.

**Types of Tape Measures**

1.  **Retractable Tape Measure:**

    *   Metal tape in a case.
    *   It snaps back into the case by itself.
    *   Used in building, woodworking, and DIY stuff.
2.  **Long Tape Measure:**

    *   Can be really long.
    *   Has a tape made of cloth, that you wind up.
    *   For measuring land, sports fields, and big construction sites.
3.  **Tailor's Measuring Tape:**

    *   Soft tape made of fabric or plastic.
    *   Used for sewing clothes and measuring bodies.
4.  **Digital Tape Measure:**

    *   Shows numbers on a screen.
    *   May remember numbers or change units, and can even measure distance
5. **Laser Tape Measure:**
    * Uses laser to measure distances.
    * Great for measuring far distances.

**What They're Made Of**

*   **Rulers:**

    *   Plastic: Light, cheap, and sometimes see-through.
    *   Wood: Old-school, strong, and good for the planet.
    *   Metal: Tough and can take heat.
*   **Tape Measures:**

    *   Steel: Strong and snaps back, but can rust.
    *   that you wind up: Good for outside because it doesn't get ruined by weather.
    *   Cloth/Plastic: Soft and bendy for sewing.

**How They Measure**

Rulers and tapes use:

*   Inches, feet, and yards.
*   Millimeters, centimeters, and meters.

Some show both at the same time.

**What to Look For**

*   **Lines and Numbers:**

    *   Should be clear and easy to see.
    *   Smaller lines mean you can measure more exactly.
*   **Hook (for Tape Measures):**

    *   Helps you hold the tape in place when you're measuring alone.
*   **Lock:**

    *   Keeps the tape from moving while you read it.
*   **Clip:**

    *   So you can carry it easily.
*   **Snapping Back:**

    *   A spring that pulls the tape back in.
*   **Magnet (on some tapes):**

    *   Sticks to metal. Can be useful.

**How to Use Them Right**

*   Line up the ruler or tape right at the start.
*   Be careful with the units—inches and centimeters are different.
*   Keep the tape tight and straight when measuring far.
*   Look straight down on rulers so you don't mess up because of the angle.

**Taking Care of Them**

*   **Rulers:**

    *   Keep them flat so they don't bend.
    *   Keep them clean so you can read them.
    *   Keep plastic rulers away from heat.
*   **Tape Measures:**

    *   Let the tape go back in slowly.
    *   Keep the tape dry so it doesn't rust.
    *   Check the hook to make sure it's not loose.
    *   Don't bend the blade.

**Be Careful!**

*   Tapes can snap back fast and hurt you.
*   Metal edges can be sharp.
*   Don't use tapes that are broken.

**Cool Facts**

*   The first ruler was about 4,000 years old in ancient Egypt.
*   The tape measure was made in the 1800s.
*   Soft tapes are better for sewing because they can bend around your body.
